### Types of Cryptocurrencies

Cryptocurrencies can be classified based on **purpose, functionality, and underlying technology**. The major types are explained below.

---

## 1. Bitcoin (Native Cryptocurrencies)

### Explanation

These are the **original cryptocurrencies** that run on their **own independent blockchain** and are mainly used as **digital money**.

### Features

- Decentralized
    
- Peer-to-peer transactions
    
- Limited supply
    

### Example

- **Bitcoin (BTC)**
    
- **Litecoin (LTC)**
    

---

## 2. Altcoins (Alternative Coins)

### Explanation

Altcoins are **all cryptocurrencies other than Bitcoin**. They usually aim to improve limitations of Bitcoin or provide additional features.

### Features

- Faster transactions
    
- Improved scalability or privacy
    
- Different consensus mechanisms
    

### Examples

- **Ethereum (ETH)**
    
- **Ripple (XRP)**
    
- **Cardano (ADA)**
    

---

## 3. Stablecoins

### Explanation

Stablecoins are cryptocurrencies designed to **maintain a stable value** by being pegged to real-world assets like **fiat currency or commodities**.

### Features

- Low volatility
    
- Used for trading and payments
    

### Types of Stablecoins

- **Fiat-backed**: USDT, USDC
    
- **Crypto-backed**: DAI
    
- **Algorithmic**: (supply-controlled)
    

---

## 4. Utility Tokens

### Explanation

Utility tokens provide **access to services or functions** within a blockchain ecosystem.

### Features

- Used for transaction fees
    
- Enable smart contract execution
    
- Power dApps
    

### Examples

- **Ether (ETH)**
    
- **BNB**
    

---

## 5. Security Tokens

### Explanation

Security tokens represent **ownership of real-world assets** such as shares, bonds, or real estate and are subject to **regulatory compliance**.

### Features

- Backed by real assets
    
- Provide dividends or profit-sharing
    

### Example

- Tokenized stocks
    
- Asset-backed tokens
    

---

## 6. Governance Tokens

### Explanation

Governance tokens allow holders to **participate in decision-making** of blockchain protocols.

### Features

- Voting rights
    
- Community governance
    

### Examples

- **UNI (Uniswap)**
    
- **AAVE**
    

---

## 7. Privacy Coins

### Explanation

Privacy coins focus on **enhancing transaction anonymity** and hiding sender, receiver, and transaction amount.

### Features

- Strong cryptographic privacy
    
- Untraceable transactions
    

### Examples

- **Monero (XMR)**
    
- **Zcash (ZEC)**
    

---

---

## Conclusion

Different types of cryptocurrencies serve different purposes, ranging from **digital money and payments** to **governance, privacy, and asset tokenization**, making the crypto ecosystem diverse and versatile.
